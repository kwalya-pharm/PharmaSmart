PharmaSmart - Medication Guide

1. Malaria

What is it? Malaria is caused by a parasite transmitted through mosquito bites. Common symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and body aches.

Medication:

  • Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL): Take as prescribed by your doctor, usually twice daily for 3 days. Always complete the dose even if you feel better.
  • Prevention: Sleep under treated mosquito nets, use insect repellents, and avoid stagnant water near your home.

2. Typhoid Fever

What is it? Typhoid is a bacterial infection spread through contaminated food or water. Symptoms include fever, stomach pain, and diarrhea or constipation.

Medication:

  • Cefixime: A common antibiotic. Take as prescribed for 7-10 days.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of clean water to stay hydrated.
  • Prevention: Ensure food is well-cooked and water is boiled or treated.

3. Respiratory Infections (Common Cold, Cough)

What is it? These are caused by viruses or bacteria and may include symptoms like cough, runny nose, sore throat, and fever.

Medication:

  • Paracetamol: For fever and pain. Take 500mg every 6 hours if needed.
  • Steam inhalation: Helps relieve congestion. Use a bowl of hot water and cover your head with a towel.
  • Fluids: Stay hydrated with warm drinks and soups.

4. Diarrhea

What is it? Diarrhea can be caused by infections, food poisoning, or contaminated water. Symptoms include loose stools and dehydration.

Medication:

  • Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS): Mix with clean water and drink frequently to prevent dehydration.
  • Zinc Tablets: Recommended for children to reduce the severity of diarrhea.
  • Prevention: Wash hands before eating and after using the toilet, and ensure food is clean and safe.

5. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

What is it? High blood pressure often has no symptoms but increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Medication:

  • Amlodipine: Take as prescribed, usually once daily.
  • Lifestyle changes: Reduce salt intake, eat more fruits and vegetables, and exercise regularly.
  • Monitoring: Check your blood pressure regularly at home or with a healthcare provider.

6. Family Planning Services

What is it? Family planning helps you decide if and when to have children. It involves different methods to prevent pregnancy and plan your family size.

Types of Family Planning Methods:

  • Contraceptive Pills: Taken daily to prevent pregnancy. They are effective but must be used consistently. Side effects: May include nausea, mood changes, or slight weight gain. Manage by taking pills at the same time daily and consulting a doctor if side effects persist.
  • Injections: Given every 2-3 months. They are discreet and long-lasting. Side effects: Irregular periods or delayed fertility return. Manage by discussing concerns with your healthcare provider.
  • Implants: Small rods placed under the skin that work for 3-5 years. Side effects: Spotting or mood changes. These usually improve with time.
  • Intrauterine Device (IUD): A device placed in the uterus, effective for 5-10 years. Side effects: Cramping or heavier periods initially. These often improve over time.
  • Condoms: Protect against pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs). Use a new one each time during sex. Side effects: Rare latex allergies. Use latex-free options if needed.
  • Tubal Ligation: A permanent method for women. It involves surgery and is irreversible. Side effects: Rare surgical complications.
  • Vasectomy: A permanent method for men. It is a simple procedure and does not affect sexual function. Side effects: Mild pain or swelling after the procedure.

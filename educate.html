Understanding Medications: What You Need to Know

Taking the right medicines is important to stay healthy, especially if you are pregnant or living with conditions like HIV or diabetes. This guide will help you understand which medicines are safe, how to take them correctly, and the dangers of misusing drugs.

Safe Medicines for Pregnant Women

  • Vitamins: Folic acid and iron are essential for a healthy pregnancy.
  • For Heartburn: Calcium carbonate is safe for use.
  • For Pain: Paracetamol is generally safe when used correctly.
  • Infections: Certain antibiotics like amoxicillin can be used safely.

Medicines for HIV

  • ARVs: Medicines like tenofovir, lamivudine, and dolutegravir (TLD) help control the virus and are safe during pregnancy.
  • Prevention: ARVs can prevent passing the virus to babies during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Managing Diabetes

  • Medicines: Insulin and metformin help control blood sugar safely, even during pregnancy.
  • Lifestyle: Healthy eating and exercise support blood sugar control.

Prescription Medicines

  • Antibiotics: Use only as prescribed to avoid resistance.
  • Painkillers: Strong medicines like morphine require strict medical supervision.

Fighting Antibiotic Resistance

  • Finish all prescribed antibiotics, even if you feel better.
  • Avoid buying antibiotics without a prescription.

How Pharmacists and Doctors Can Help

Always consult a pharmacist or doctor if you are unsure about any medicine. Following their advice ensures your safety and the effectiveness of treatments.
